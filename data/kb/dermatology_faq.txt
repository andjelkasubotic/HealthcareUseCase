## faq-001 — Portal response times
Our dermatology portal is monitored during business hours (Mon–Fri, 8am–5pm).
Routine messages receive a clinician review within 1–2 business days.
Urgent messages are reviewed within 24–72 hours.
Do not use the portal for life-threatening emergencies — call 911 or go to the ER.

## faq-002 — Appointment scheduling
To reschedule or cancel, reply with your preferred dates or call the front desk.
Same-day appointment requests are reviewed but not guaranteed.
Include your full name and date of birth when requesting schedule changes.

## faq-003 — Billing and insurance
For copay, statement, or insurance questions, our billing team will respond within 3 business days.
We cannot discuss specific charges without verifying your identity.
Do not send credit card numbers through the portal.

## faq-004 — Prescription refills (no red flags)
For routine refills of existing dermatology medications with no new symptoms,
a clinician will review your chart and respond within 2 business days.
If you have new or worsening symptoms, describe them in your message.

## faq-005 — Mild rash or itch (routine)
For mild, stable rashes or itch without fever, facial swelling, or rapid spreading,
a clinician will review your message and recommend next steps.
Do not start, stop, or change medications without clinician approval.
We cannot diagnose or prescribe through the portal message alone.

## faq-006 — Changing mole or new lesion (urgent)
Reports of a new or changing pigmented lesion, growing mole, or non-healing spot
are treated as clinically concerning and prioritized for clinician review within 24–72 hours.
A clinician will determine whether an in-person visit or biopsy is needed.
We will not diagnose skin cancer through a portal message.

## faq-007 — Severe flare of chronic condition (urgent)
If you describe a severe flare of eczema, psoriasis, or similar condition,
a clinician will review promptly and contact you about treatment options.
Do not adjust steroid or immunosuppressant doses on your own.

## faq-008 — Emergency escalation (emergent)
If you report facial or lip swelling, difficulty breathing, fever with a rapidly spreading rash,
mucosal involvement, or signs of a severe drug reaction, the message is flagged for same-day review.
For immediate danger, call 911. A clinician will follow up as soon as possible.

## faq-009 — Post-operative wound concerns (urgent/emergent)
Increasing pain, spreading redness, pus, or fever after a skin procedure requires prompt review.
Describe when the procedure was done and what changes you notice.
For severe pain or fever, seek emergency care and notify us through the portal.

## faq-010 — What clinicians will not do via portal
Clinicians do not provide diagnoses, prescribe new medications, or change dosages
based on portal messages alone. All replies are drafts for clinician review before sending.
Your message helps us triage and prepare a response — it is not a substitute for an exam.
